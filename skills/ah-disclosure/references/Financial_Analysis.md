# Financial analysis patterns

Acquire the relevant statements, notes, cash-flow reconciliation, accounting policies, and MD&A. Preserve original units and periods. Let the LLM define the analysis and classifications; let the Kit verify arithmetic and evidence links.

## Cash flow and financing

- Tie indirect CFO from NI/PBT through non-cash adjustments and working-capital movements.
- Tie opening cash through CFO, CFI, CFF, and FX to ending cash.
- Separate expense recognition from payment, and principal from interest.
- Distinguish cash capex, asset additions, acquisitions, disposals, leases, and non-cash changes.
- Reconcile financing liabilities using cash flows, FX, fair value, acquisitions, disposals, and other non-cash movements.
- Do not equate finance expense with financing cash paid.

## Effective tax rate

- Tie the tax reconciliation to tax expense and PBT in both amount and rate form.
- Separate statutory-rate effects, jurisdiction mix, permanent items, tax losses, credits, incentives, and prior-year adjustments.
- Reconcile current tax to tax payable and tax paid only when the required roll-forward is disclosed.
- Reconcile deferred tax through P&L, OCI, FX, acquisitions/disposals, rate changes, offsets, and reclassifications.
- Classify each cross-statement link as direct tie-out, auxiliary tie-out, estimate, or unavailable.

## Management balance sheet, DuPont, and ROIC

- Show the statutory statement before management reclassification.
- Classify second-level accounts into operating, financing, investment, and equity categories.
- Keep statutory working capital separate from management NWC.
- Calculate operating current assets/liabilities, NWC, net operating long-term assets, NOA/capital employed, financial assets, financing liabilities, and NFO.
- Bridge `NOA = equity + NFO` and disclose ambiguous classifications.
- Tie DuPont ROE to parent NI and average parent equity.
- Treat NOPAT and ROIC as management estimates unless directly disclosed; state the capital convention.

## Equity incentives and cross-document work

- Separate grant terms, vesting, exercise price, valuation method and inputs, expense, cash proceeds, dilution, and outstanding awards.
- Use option-pricing calculations only when required inputs and units are evidenced.
- Across documents, align periods, currencies, units, standards, consolidation scope, and document versions.
- Separate rounding differences from definition conflicts and surface same-metric conflicts.
